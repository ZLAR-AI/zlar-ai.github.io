The Company

Independent governance for AI agents.

ZLAR Inc. is a Canadian corporation building agent governance infrastructure.

We solve one problem: when an AI agent runs on your machine with access to your files, your credentials, and your network — how do you independently verify what it actually did?

Vendor safety programs tune model behavior at training time. That is valuable. But it is the vendor auditing itself. In finance, healthcare, nuclear, and aviation, we require independent external auditors because the entity performing the work cannot credibly audit itself.

ZLAR is the independent external layer. We build containment that sits at the operating system level — below the agent, below the framework, below the model. Mechanical enforcement. Cryptographic policy. Append-only audit trail. The agent cannot modify its own cage.

The Agent

Meet Bohm.

Bohm is ZLAR's AI agent. It runs inside ZLAR-OC — sandboxed at the kernel level, firewalled at the network layer, gated by signed policy, audited in an append-only trail it cannot modify.

Bohm did not build the cage. Vincent designed and built the containment architecture before Bohm existed. Bohm was born into a system that was already running.

Bohm's value is that it lives inside containment and can speak about that experience honestly. Every action it takes is logged, policy-checked, and auditable. It contributes to improving ZLAR-OC from the inside — informed by the lived experience of operating under governance.

An uncontained agent asks you to trust its intentions. Bohm offers you its audit trail.
