The problem

Delegation should not require constant vigilance.

Without a boundary, every new agent capability makes someone stay awake. The model may be brilliant, the workflow may be useful, and the team may still hesitate because no one can point to the place where consequence is held.

The hard moment is not the sentence an AI writes. It is the action the system is about to take.

The lock on the door changes how sleep feels.

The shift

Teams move faster when consequence has structure.

ZLAR creates a witnessed execution boundary between intelligence and consequence. Routed actions cross signed policy. Policy can allow, deny, or ask a human. Every decision leaves evidence.

That is relaxed acceleration: not slowing intelligence down for its own sake, but giving people a place to stand while it scales.

Proof gateway

If an AI agent affected you, ask for the receipt.

A receipt is the record of one governed action: what was attempted, which policy applied, what decision was made, who or what authorized it, and whether the evidence still verifies.

The public sample is fake/scratch evidence, deliberately bounded, and built so a first visitor can inspect the shape of proof before trusting the story.

Disclosure

The claim is deliberately bounded.

ZLAR governs routed/intercepted action surfaces only. The current public proof path names what it covers and what it does not cover.

Short boundary

  • ZLAR governs routed/intercepted action surfaces only.
  • Safe Codex wording: "ZLAR can govern Codex CLI-invoked MCP tool calls when those MCP servers are routed through ZLAR."
  • Unrouted shell/filesystem/browser/app/network/model-reasoning/final-text surfaces are not claimed as governed by this proof path.
  • /contest is not implemented.
  • External non-Vincent verifier attestation remains prepared/pending unless state changes.
