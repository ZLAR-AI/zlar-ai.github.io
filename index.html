What ZLAR is

A deterministic boundary where intention becomes action.

An agent may generate plans, code, messages, edits, requests, and tool calls. Consequence crosses into the world through a separate, signed, deterministic authority.

ZLAR evaluates routed actions against human-signed policy, allows routine actions, denies forbidden actions, routes consequential actions to a human when required, and produces evidence that the governance event happened.

ZLAR is not a smarter judge inside the model. It is the boundary outside the model.

The absence of AI in the enforcement path is the point.

Governed Action Receipt

If an AI agent affected you, ask for the receipt.

A receipt is the portable record of a governed action: what was attempted, what policy applied, who or what authorized it, when the decision happened, and whether the record still verifies.

The affected person may never have been in the approval loop. The receipt is how they can ask what happened anyway.

Coverage Report

Show where the boundary held and where it ended.

A receipt proves one governed decision. A coverage report explains the perimeter of the run. It names the surfaces that were routed, the bypass sentinels that were blocked, the limitations that were disclosed, and the surfaces outside this proof path.

Routed

Evidence exists that this action surface crossed the ZLAR gate.

Blocked

A bypass route or unsafe decision was refused by the proof path.

Disclosed

The limitation is named so it cannot masquerade as coverage.

Out of scope

The surface needs another interception or deployment control.

Sealed Mode

A stronger deployment posture closes the paths around the gate.

ZLAR governs routed actions. A serious deployment also removes or blocks the paths that do not cross the boundary. That stronger posture is Sealed Mode.

Sealed Mode pairs the gate with sandbox profiles, network egress controls, filesystem allowlists, controlled shell access, proxied MCP access, read-only policy and gate files, external key custody, and receipt witnessing or external log anchoring.

Boundaries

The claim is powerful because it is bounded.

The website can speak plainly in founder voice. The disclosure still has to be exact.

v3.3.15 claim boundary

  • ZLAR governs routed/intercepted action surfaces only.
  • Safe Codex wording: "ZLAR can govern Codex CLI-invoked MCP tool calls when those MCP servers are routed through ZLAR."
  • Unrouted shell/filesystem/browser/app/network/model-reasoning/final-text surfaces are not claimed as governed by this proof path.
  • /contest is not implemented.
  • External non-Vincent verifier attestation remains prepared/pending unless state changes.
