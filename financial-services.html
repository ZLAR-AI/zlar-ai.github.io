For Banks / Financial Services

The credential boundary is not the execution boundary.

Financial institutions know how to authenticate users, services, and workloads. Agentic AI creates a second question: after authentication, what is the agent allowed to do, who stood behind the action, and can the record be verified without the vendor?

Schedule a briefing Read the CAISI submission
The gap

Authentication says who is present. It does not say what should happen.

Agents can act continuously across accounts, workflows, files, and systems. The governance problem is not only identity. It is execution: which action crossed the boundary, which policy applied, and which authority allowed or denied it.

A vendor dashboard can be useful for operations. It is not the same as a portable record of action-level authority.

The receipt is the control surface auditors can inspect after the action.

The primitive

A receipt binds action, policy, authority, and time.

Action

What was attempted?

A governed action receipt starts at the specific action, not a broad system claim.

Decision

What decided it?

Policy may allow, deny, or ask a named human before consequence proceeds.

Evidence

Can it be checked later?

Receipts and audit chains are designed to make governance events inspectable.

Where to start

Begin away from core customer impact.

The right first conversation is a bounded internal workflow, a routed MCP/tool surface, or a non-customer-impacting action path where risk, compliance, and engineering can define allow, deny, and ask together.

Boundary

  • ZLAR governs routed/intercepted action surfaces only.
  • Receipts support action-level review; they do not prove the action was correct, lawful, fair, or wise.
  • ZLAR does not replace institution-owned controls, custody, retention, or supervisory judgment.
  • External non-Vincent verifier attestation remains prepared/pending unless state changes.
