Founder Note

ZLAR exists so people stay present when AI starts to act.

AI can move now. Before it does something real, there should be a door with a rule, a real yes when needed, and proof afterward.

Contact ZLAR See a sample receipt
ZLAR exists so people can keep a real place at the door before AI does something real.

That is the whole center of it.

AI can change files, call tools, move data, run commands, and start workflows. I want those actions to have a door before they reach the world.

The door is not another AI making a vibe check. It is a rule.

The rule says: this is allowed, this is blocked, or this needs a person to say yes.

The AI does not get to invent the rule while it is trying to act.

The yes has to be real

I care about the moment when ZLAR asks a person.

A person needs time to think. They need room to say no. They should not be buried in requests until the only way out is clicking yes. That is not control. That is fatigue with a nicer interface.

A fast no can be real. A fast yes can be dangerous.

When the action matters, ZLAR is built to make the yes real: separate from the AI, tied to the rule, and recorded afterward.

The action leaves proof

After the decision, ZLAR leaves a receipt.

The receipt answers plain questions. What did the AI try to do? What rule was used? Was it allowed? Was it blocked? Did a person say yes? Can we still prove what happened?

That proof matters for the team using AI. It also matters for people affected by the action, especially if they were never asked.

The doorway is honest about its limits

ZLAR does not govern every AI action everywhere.

It governs the paths routed through it. That limit is not a footnote. It is the honest shape of the product.

A serious deployment has to close bypass paths around the door. The rule only matters if the action actually has to pass through it.

What I am building for

AI can move.

People stay present.

Actions leave proof.

That is ZLAR.

Exact scope

  • ZLAR governs routed/intercepted action surfaces only.
  • Safe Codex wording: "ZLAR can govern Codex CLI-invoked MCP tool calls when those MCP servers are routed through ZLAR."
  • Unrouted shell/filesystem/browser/app/network/model-reasoning/final-text surfaces are not claimed as governed by the current proof path.
  • /contest is not implemented.
  • External non-Vincent verifier attestation remains prepared/pending unless state changes.
