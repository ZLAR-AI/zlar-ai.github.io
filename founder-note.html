Founder Note

I built ZLAR because AI is starting to do real things.

It can change files, call tools, move data, and start workflows. Before it does that, it needs a doorway.

Contact ZLAR See a sample receipt

I do not think the hard part is AI talking.

The hard part is AI doing.

A sentence can be wrong and still be a sentence. An action can change a file, move a record, send a message, trigger a workflow, or affect a person.

That is a different kind of problem.

ZLAR is the doorway AI must pass through before it does something real.

The doorway is not another AI making a vibe check. It is a rule.

The rule says: this is allowed, this is blocked, or this needs a person to say yes.

The AI does not get to invent the rule while it is trying to act.

The yes has to be real

I care a lot about the moment when ZLAR asks a person.

A person needs time to think. They need room to say no. They should not be buried in requests until the only way out is clicking yes. That is not control. That is fatigue with a nicer interface.

A fast no can be real. A fast yes can be dangerous.

So when the action matters, ZLAR is built to make the yes more real: separate from the AI, tied to a rule, and recorded afterward.

The receipt matters

After the decision, ZLAR leaves a receipt.

The receipt should answer plain questions. What did the AI try to do? What rule was used? Was it allowed? Was it blocked? Did a person say yes? Can we still prove what happened?

That record matters for operators. It also matters for people who were affected by the action and were never in the approval loop.

The boundary is limited on purpose

I am not claiming ZLAR governs every AI action everywhere.

It governs the paths routed through it. That limit is not a footnote. It is the honest shape of the product.

A strong deployment has to close bypass paths around the doorway. The rule only matters if the action actually has to pass through it.

That is ZLAR

AI can move.

People stay present.

Actions leave proof.

Exact scope

  • ZLAR governs routed/intercepted action surfaces only.
  • Safe Codex wording: "ZLAR can govern Codex CLI-invoked MCP tool calls when those MCP servers are routed through ZLAR."
  • Unrouted shell/filesystem/browser/app/network/model-reasoning/final-text surfaces are not claimed as governed by the current proof path.
  • /contest is not implemented.
  • External non-Vincent verifier attestation remains prepared/pending unless state changes.
